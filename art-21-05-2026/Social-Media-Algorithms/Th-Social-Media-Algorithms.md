# The Social Media Math Problem 📱🧮

Why do platforms push controversy instead of important discussions? It is simple math. 🧵

1/ Algorithms are built for one thing: Engagement. They want to keep your eyes on the screen to sell ads. 

2/ What drives engagement? Outrage. Anger makes us comment, share, and stare. Nuanced, important discussions don't trigger the same fast dopamine hit. ⚡

3/ How bad is it? A 2025 *Science* study showed that algorithms are literally driving us apart. When researchers tweaked feeds to hide hostile content, users became significantly less polarized in just ONE week. 🤯

4/ The hidden formula isn't about giving you what you *want*, it is about giving you what you *can't look away from*. 

5/ We need "bridging" algorithms that reward consensus, not just conflict. Until then, remember: Your feed is a funhouse mirror, not reality. 🪞
