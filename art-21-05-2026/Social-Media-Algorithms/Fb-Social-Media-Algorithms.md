**Title:** Why Your Feed is Always Angry 😡📱

Ever notice how the internet feels like it is constantly fighting? It is not an accident. Social media platforms use a hidden formula to keep you scrolling. 

Here is why they push controversy over important discussions:

1️⃣ **Engagement is King:** Algorithms track what makes you stop scrolling. Anger and outrage make people comment and share more than calm, factual news does. 📈
2️⃣ **The Outrage Loop:** When the algorithm sees you reacting to controversial topics, it feeds you more of them, trapping you in a bubble of hostility. 🔄
3️⃣ **Nuance is "Boring":** Deep, important discussions take time to read. The algorithm prefers quick, emotional reactions. 💤

**How bad is this for society?**
Pretty bad. A massive 2025 study in *Science* proved that these algorithms actively make us hate opposing groups more. When researchers temporarily hid angry posts from users' feeds, their views of the "other side" improved dramatically in just one week! 🤯

We are not naturally this divided—the math is just pushing us apart. The next time you see something that makes your blood boil online, ask yourself: *Did the algorithm put this here just to keep my attention?* 🤔
