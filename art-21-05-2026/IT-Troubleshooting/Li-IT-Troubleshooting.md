The true cost of IT downtime isn't just lost revenue—it's the hidden emotional toll on your engineering team. 📊

When critical systems fail, the pressure mismatch between business demands for instant uptime and the painstaking reality of proper root cause analysis creates an unsustainable environment. We've all been there: staring at a wall of logs, tracing a ridiculously obscure bug through undocumented legacy code, while stakeholders ask for ETA updates every five minutes. 

According to a 2023 report by Atlassian, 42% of IT professionals experience severe burnout directly tied to the pressure of resolving critical incidents under tight SLAs. This isn't just a technical problem; it's a leadership crisis.

When we treat troubleshooting merely as "fixing what's broken" instead of investing in technical debt reduction and proper documentation, we set our teams up for failure. 

The incredible satisfaction of finally resolving a complex issue after hours of frustration is undeniable, but it shouldn't require heroics. 🚀 

Leaders must shift focus:
✅ Prioritize comprehensive documentation
✅ Allocate sprint time specifically for refactoring legacy systems
✅ Shield engineers from stakeholder panic during active incidents
