**Slide 4 — Reusable Tech**
**Alt Text:** A Falcon 9 rocket booster landing precisely on a drone ship at sea.
The Power of Reusable Tech ♻️
Private companies have achieved something government agencies struggled with: reusability. 
Landing and reusing rockets has drastically slashed the cost of going to space, making wild business models suddenly viable. 📉

**Slide 5 — Spinoffs**
**Alt Text:** An astronaut helmet reflecting a modern clean-energy city on Earth.
Spinoff Technology 🌍
Space is the ultimate pressure cooker for innovation. 
Trying to keep humans alive in a vacuum forces breakthroughs in energy efficiency, water purification, and advanced materials—tech that ends up solving problems down here on Earth. 💡

**Slide 6 — Backup Plan**
**Alt Text:** An hourglass with Earth in the top half and Mars in the bottom half.
The Ultimate Backup Plan 🧬
For visionaries like Elon Musk, making humanity "multi-planetary" is about existential risk. 
It’s essentially creating a backup drive for human consciousness in case of a global catastrophe on Earth. ☄️

**Caption:**
Why are the world’s wealthiest people pouring their fortunes into space exploration? It’s not just an ego trip—it’s the ultimate long-term bet on humanity’s survival and a trillion-dollar frontier. 🚀 

While it's easy to criticize the massive price tags, the push into orbit is driving incredible innovation. By drastically lowering the cost of rocket launches, we are entering a new era of space commercialization that will bring global internet access and new spinoff technologies to everyday life on Earth. 🌍✨
