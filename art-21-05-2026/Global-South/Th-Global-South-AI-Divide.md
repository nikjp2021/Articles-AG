**Title:** The AI Divide: Who Actually Gets Rich?

We need to talk about who actually gets rich from AI. Are we just creating a massive new tech divide? Let's discuss. 👇

1/ Everyone is hyping up how AI will add trillions to the global economy. But look at the data: almost all of that wealth is consolidating in the Global North. We are actively watching a new digital divide form in real-time. 📉👀

2/ The **UNCTAD Technology and Innovation Report 2025** lays it out clearly. The Global South is facing a massive "compute" deficit. You can't build native AI models if you don't have the data centers or the energy grid to power them. 🔋

3/ Right now, many emerging economies are being forced into a dependency trap—relying on foreign AI infrastructure and models trained entirely on Western data. If an AI doesn't even speak your language, how is it going to solve your local problems? 🗣️🚫

4/ The good news? Grassroots tech movements in places like India, Africa, and Latin America are pushing back. They are building "decolonized" AI—models trained on indigenous knowledge and local dialects. They aren't waiting for permission. 🔥💪

5/ If we don't include the Global South in AI governance right now, we risk automating inequality on a global scale. We need less monopoly and more open-source, local innovation. What’s your take? 🌍🗣️
