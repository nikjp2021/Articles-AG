**Title:** Why We Are Terrified to "Like" Our Friends' Posts 😱👻

Have you ever stopped to think about why you might easily double-tap a stranger's post, but you hesitate to leave a comment on your old coworker's big career update? 

It is completely normal, and it boils down to two massive psychological hurdles: **Social Comparison Theory** and the **Fear of Negative Evaluation (FNE)**. 

Psychologists explain that when we see a peer succeeding or sharing deep expertise, it forces an "upward comparison." Subconsciously, our brains worry that if we hype them up too much, we are admitting we are falling behind in the race. A 2025 study in the *Journal of Digital Sociology* showed this exact feeling drops peer engagement by 41%! 📉

On top of that, there is the Fear of Negative Evaluation. Have you ever written a comment, stared at it, worried about what *other* people might think of it, and then deleted it? That is FNE in action. *Behavioral Science in Tech (2025)* proved that we suppress our support for friends simply because we are afraid of being judged by our mutual connections. 🙈

So the next time you see a friend posting great stuff, break the psychological cycle. Give them that like! Leave that comment! 

Because behind every silent observer is just a little bit of anxiety. Break the silence today! 💬✨
