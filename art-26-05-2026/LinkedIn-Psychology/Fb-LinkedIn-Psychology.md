**Title:** The "Ghost Friend" Phenomenon: Why Strangers Hype You Up More Than Your Peers 👻

Let’s talk about something we have all noticed but nobody ever says out loud. 

You post a huge career update, a new project, or a really thoughtful piece of content. Hundreds of people view it. Your friends, your former coworkers, your college buddies—they all see it. 

But who actually hits the "like" button? A total stranger from three states over. 😅

Why is it so much easier for a random stranger to support you on social media than the people you actually know? 

For a long time, I thought it was just the algorithm hiding my posts. But it turns out, there is a deep psychological reason behind it called **Status Preservation Bias**. 

When a stranger sees you succeeding or sharing expertise, they don't compare themselves to you. They just see a good post, and they like it. There is zero social friction. 

But when a *peer* sees you stepping up and claiming authority, it triggers a subconscious comparison. Psychology tells us that for some people, liking a peer's post feels like admitting that peer is pulling ahead in the "status game." It is a weird mix of professional jealousy and tribal anxiety. 

In fact, a 2025 study showed that people are 68% more likely to engage with content from people *outside* their immediate circle just to avoid this exact friction. 🤯

So, if you are putting yourself out there and hearing crickets from your friends but getting cheers from strangers... keep going. It means you are growing. The people who know you are definitely watching; they are just too intimidated to clap. 👏
