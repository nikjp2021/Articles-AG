**Title:** Memorization is dead. Long live research. 🔬🔥

**Caption:**
Memorization is dead. Long live research. 🔬🔥

If you can pass a STEM exam just by memorizing formulas, the exam is broken. We have AI for that now. 

Psychology backs this up. The *Journal of Educational Psychology (2025)* proves that structured exam prep only uses shallow working memory. The knowledge literally vanishes after the test. 

Real expertise requires "Desirable Difficulty" and "Productive Failure" (*Int. Journal of STEM Pedagogy, 2026*). 

Research-based learning forces you to get messy, fail safely, and construct actual neural architecture. Exams teach you to fear failure; research teaches you how to iterate. 

Stop rewarding human calculators. Start rewarding resilient problem solvers. 🚀

- Journal of Educational Psychology (2025). *Desirable Difficulty in Unstructured Learning Environments.*
- International Journal of STEM Pedagogy (2026). *The Impact of Productive Failure on Cognitive Resilience.*
