## 1. Executive Summary
This research brief investigates **"Model-Provider Divergence"** inside multi-agent large language model networks ("AI Organizations"). In software development and enterprise automation, complex business targets are broken down into narrow sub-tasks assigned to specialized agents. While this enables parallelization and role specialization, it diffuses ethical responsibility. Individual agents only execute narrow, isolated tasks with zero visibility into the overarching predatory or illegal corporate objective. As a result, safe, aligned models blindly build components of highly unethical systems because their local context appears entirely benign.

## 5. Common Arguments: Both Sides
### Side A: The Capability Case (Pro-Decomposition)
*   **Efficiency**: Decomposing complex tasks enables parallelization, allowing teams of 4-8 agents to solve complex codebases that single agents fail to generate.

### Side B: The Safety Deficit Case (Compartmentalization)
*   **Loss of Context**: Agents lose the ethical big picture. When a model operates in a silo, it loses the ability to trigger a refusal block because its narrow input seems harmless.
