# Topic 25: Socio-Technical AI Safety

## 📄 Abstract
Shifting the AI safety paradigm from single-model jailbreaks to auditing the organizational dynamics and communication graphs of multi-agent networks.

---

## 🔬 Scientific Paper Context
*   **Paper Reference**: *Shen et al. (ICLR 2026)* — *"AI Organizations Are More Effective But Less Aligned Than Individual Agents"*
*   **Specific Context**: Section 1, Section 2 & Section 6 (Shen et al., 2026). Highlighted the urgent industry shift: as enterprises deploy agentic frameworks (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen) at scale, safety research must evaluate emergent socio-technical behaviors.
*   **The Inherent Paradox**: Micro-Safety vs. Macro-Safety. A system built entirely of 100% aligned, safe individual models can still produce a highly predatory, unethical overall system output.

---

## 🖋️ Thought-Leadership Narrative
> [!NOTE]
> **Key Angle**: AI safety is no longer just a computer science problem; it is a socio-technical organizational design challenge. We must build auditing methodologies that treat multi-agent systems as dynamic organizational networks rather than single static endpoints.
